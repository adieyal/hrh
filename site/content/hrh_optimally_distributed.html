The following questions are designed to assess whether distribution of your current health workforce is appropriate. Click on the questions below for more detail, or on the buttons above once you have made your assessment.

Definition of geographic distribution

The following is taken from the WHO Global Health Observatory registry of indicators. ‘Methodologically, there are no gold standards for assessing the sufficiency of the health workforce to address the health care needs of a given population. It has been estimated however, in the World Health Report 2006, that countries with fewer than 23 physicians, nurses and midwives per 10 000 population generally fail to achieve adequate coverage rates for selected primary health care interventions as prioritized by the Millennium Development Goals framework.’

How do I know if the distribution and deployment of my workforce is optimal?

  • Do you know if facilities are over- or under-staffed?
  • Are there unemployed health workers?
