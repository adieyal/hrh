Workforce Indicators of Staffing Needs (WISN) tool

Description of tool

The WISN method is a human resource management tool. It provides health managers a systematic way to make staffing decisions. The method determines the number of health workers required to cope with the workload of a given health facility and assesses the workload pressure of health workers. It enables answers to the following questions:

  • How many health workers of a particular cadre do I need in a certain health facility
  • Are health workers deployed based on demand for health service?

Prerequisites

  • Support from senior directors & managers with responsibility for HRH decisions, and commitment from other stakeholders (eg professional associations and unions)
  • Clarity on why you want to use the WISN method and for what decisions.
  • Annual workload statistics that are of acceptable quality and up to date.
  • Resources

How long does it take

Depending on the scope of the WISN implementation exercise, it could take from 6 months to a year or more to implement the process in full.

Examples of implementation

Case studies are available from Indonesia, Mozambique and Uganda.
