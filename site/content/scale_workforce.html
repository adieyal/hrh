Scaling up your stock of human resources for health must be done with a clear understanding of the national goals and objectives for the health system and for health services as the juncture between the health system and the population. An essential step of an HRH planning exercise is to make the most of the existing health workers through ensuring that they are adequately deployed, highly productive, delivering quality care and are retained within the health service. The following questions all highlight considerations relevant to scaling up your health workforce:

  • Are you making the most of existing health workers?
  • Is there a current Human Resources for Health Plan in place that fits with the UHC strategy?
  • Does the plan include an analysis of trends and future gaps?
  • Does the plan include costed scenarios with options for improving performance and producing additional health workforce?
  • Are financial resources available (both for investments and future recurrent costs)?
  • How do I increase the number of health professionals?
  • Do new recruits have jobs to go to?
